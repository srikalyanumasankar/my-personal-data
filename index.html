My Theories

Critique of the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI)

The Many-Worlds Interpretation suggests that every quantum possibility is realized in separate branches of the universe. For example, in Schrödinger's cat experiment, one universe has a live cat, and another has a dead cat. While this is an elegant solution to avoid wave function collapse, I raise the following critical question:

If all possibilities exist in parallel universes, why can't we interact with alternate versions of ourselves? If my alternate self exists in a parallel universe, there should theoretically be some observable effect or interaction, even if indirect. The complete lack of such evidence undermines the plausibility of this interpretation.

Reinterpreting Schrödinger's Cat Experiment

The Schrödinger's cat thought experiment presents a cat in a superposition of being alive and dead until observed. However, I argue that the cat itself serves as an observer.

Inside the box, numerous quantum interactions occur within the cat's body (e.g., biochemical reactions). These interactions effectively "observe" the state of the cat, breaking the superposition long before a human opens the box. Observation doesn't require human consciousness; it merely involves a physical interaction that localizes the wave function.
